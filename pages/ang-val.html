English Grammar Essentials

Understanding key grammar structures is essential for success in the English exam. It helps you understand texts better and express yourself clearly.

First Conditional (Pirmā tipa nosacījuma teikumi)

Definition

We use the First Conditional to talk about real and possible situations in the future. It describes what will happen if a certain condition is met.

Formula

If + [Present Simple clause], + [Future Simple (will + base verb) clause]

OR

[Future Simple (will + base verb) clause] + if + [Present Simple clause] (No comma needed here)

Key Points

Remember the tense combination: Present Simple in the 'if' clause (the condition) and Future Simple (will + verb) in the main clause (the result).

Use a comma after the 'if' clause when it comes first. Do not use a comma if the 'if' clause comes second.

Common mistake: Do not use 'will' in the 'if' clause. (Incorrect: If I will see him...)

Passive Voice - Simple Tenses (Ciešamā kārta - vienkāršie laiki)

Definition

We use the passive voice when the focus is on the action itself or the object that receives the action, rather than the person or thing performing the action (the agent). It's often used when the agent is unknown, unimportant, or obvious. We'll focus on the Present Simple Passive and Past Simple Passive.

Formula

[Object of the active sentence] + [Correct form of 'to be' (am/is/are for Present; was/were for Past)] + [Past Participle (V3)] + (by Agent)

Key Points

The structure is always be + Past Participle (V3). Make sure you know the Past Participle form of common verbs (e.g., write -> written, break -> broken, make -> made, see -> seen).

Choose the correct form of 'be' depending on the tense (Present or Past) and the subject (singular or plural).

You only include by + agent if it's important to mention who or what performed the action.

Reported Speech - Statements (Netiešā runa - apgalvojuma teikumi)

Definition

Reported speech (also called indirect speech) is used to tell someone what another person said, but without quoting their exact words. When reporting statements, we often need to change tenses, pronouns, and time/place expressions.

Formula

[Reporting Verb (e.g., said, told)] + (that) + [Subject] + [Backshifted Verb Tense]

Tense backshift

Tense Backshift: You usually move the verb tense one step back into the past.

Present Simple (I am) -> Past Simple (He said he was)

Present Continuous (I am watching) -> Past Continuous (He said he was watching)

Past Simple (I went) -> Past Perfect (He said he had gone)

Present Perfect (I have finished) -> Past Perfect (He said he had finished)

Pronoun Changes

Pronouns often change depending on who is reporting. (I -> he/she, my -> his/her, you -> me/him/her/us/them, your -> my/his/her/our/their, we -> they)

Time/Place Changes

Words referring to time and place might change. (now -> then, today -> that day, yesterday -> the day before, tomorrow -> the next day/the following day, here -> there, this -> that, these -> those)
